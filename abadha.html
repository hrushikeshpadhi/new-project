Augmentation of Basic Amenities and Development of Heritage and Architecture at Puri

The holy town of Puri is of enormous interest to tourists and hosts a floating population of more than 50,000 per day. It is estimated that at Puri, 500 to 1000 Cars, 1000 bikes and more than 500 buses ply on an everyday basis. One truly gets a whiff of the rich Odishi heritage by simply walking on the streets of Puri. It is also home to a wide array of interrelated crafts such as stone carving; the traditional miniature paintings (Patta) that refelect centuries of dedication and perfection; sophisticated Odishi textiles, dynamic visual and performing arts; and even the distinctive cuisine.

Being a small town with narrow and congested lanes it has posed a massive challenge for planned development. In an attempt to rid the cultural landmarks of congestion and truly make Puri a Global Heritage hub, Sate Govt. has taken up this ambitious infrastructure augmentation with a preservationist mindset. As part of the augmentation, besides the notable development projects several other beautification initiatives are also being conceived. All development projects are being undertaken in consultation with Jagannath Temple Administration Trust.

Phase I

Infrastructure Projects

Shree Setu

Shree Setu, a 60 meter wide bridge would be built with a cost estimate of 180 Crore. The bridge would cover a total length of 1.5 kms and would have an Iconic Interchange connecting NH to Bada Danda. A trumpet intersection with access control would lead straight to the Jagannath Ballav Mutt. In addition, the bridge would have viewpoints offering sights of Shree Mandir and Musa River.

Multi-Level Car Parking at Jail Road

The proposed ultilevel Car Parking (MLCP) at Jail Road, would be spread over 3 acres and would primarily be dedicated for vehicle parking and vending space. The ground and first level have provision for 355 vending spaces. The cost estimate of this facility is 68 Crs. The capacity is 135 Bike Parking facility partly in the Ground and First Floor. In addition, there would be dedicated car parking for about 600 vehicles in the Second & Third Level along with part of Ground & First Level.

Jail Road being the first entry point for tourists, this Multi Level Car Parking would have locational advantage by helping reduce congestion in Puri. Tourists could park bikes and cars at ease and board eco-friendly battery operated vehicles for sightseeing.

Jagannath Ballav Mutt – A World Class Pilgrim Centre

Jagannath Ballav Pilgrim Centre (JBPC), the pilgrim centre is being augmented as a heritage structure conforming to the cultural ambience of Puri. The 190 crore project would be spread over 5.5 acres and house facilities such as a tourist centre, a 10,000 sqm meditation space, 200 sqm multi modal hub, a multi-level car parking and shopping outlets showcasing rich heritage of Odishi Art & Culture.

Phase II

Five projects have been approved with an approximate estimate of Rs 495 Crore. These projects would be executed by OBCC under Works Department. The Detailed Project Reports (DPR) for these projects have been initiated.

Development & Preservation of Heritage Mathas at Puri

The old Mathas (Hindu monasteries) at Puri need special efforts for preservation and redevelopment. To support this initiative, State Government would extend an initial corpus of Rs 100 Crore. The efforts would roll out in coordination with Collector, Puri and would be funded under ABADHA scheme.

Beautification of Water Bodies at Puri
Revival of MUSA River

This plan includes revival of the historic course of river Musa and connecting it with river Mangala through greenways and network of lakes. Development of about 4.8 kms of river course till its confluence would be taken up with an estimated project cost of Rs. 85 Crore. The water would be replenished by restoring natural ecology through removal of weeds, dredging and de-silting. Issues related to effluents and pollutants would also be checked. Further, river way navigation and accessibility would be promoted along with provision of recreational activities in the riverbank.

Development of Puri Lake and other Heritage Lakes in Town

In addition to salvaging Musa, an estimate of Rs. 35 Crores has been earmarked for developing the existing fisheries pond of 24 acres under the Fisheries Department. Environmental up-gradation of buffer zones though plantation along rivers and lakes with an increased access for pedestrians would be ensured.

Heritage Lakes at Puri with religious significance such as Sweta Ganga, Markanda, Narendra & Indradyumna would be also revived as places of interest for public and tourists at large.

Athar Nullah Heritage Development

This historically significant site would be developed as an iconic pedestrian heritage bridge with the support of ASI. Under this project, initiatives such as Lighting of the bridge to enhance its architectural heritage features; signage and public seating area with landscaping, paved paths for pedestrians, kiosks and play area for children would be developed at an estimated cost of Rs. 5 Cr.

Govt. Accomodation Development Programme (GADP)

A smart Urban Development and Planning Project

Bhubaneswar is at the throes of a great change. As development intensifies, accompanying need for space is among the top most priorities of the Government. Needless to mention that the city has to grow vertically to accommodate its ever increasing population of workers, techies and especially government employees. In view of this, improvement of Government Accommodations in Bhubaneswar is among the top priorities of OBCC. To increase the availability of government quarters in Bhubaneswar, a comprehensive Government Accommodation Plan (GADP) is being implemented with OBCC as the nodal agency. A total of 200 Crs has been earmarked for these projects. It deserves mention that the maintenance of these government quarters project for the next 10 years has been factored into the project proposal.
